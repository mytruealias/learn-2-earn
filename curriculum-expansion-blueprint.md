  Version 1.0 — April 2026

  ## 1. Curriculum Strategy Overview

  ### Philosophy

  Learn 2 Earn is not an app. It is a structured life-change system delivered in a mobile-first interface. Every lesson must answer one question from the learner's perspective: What can I do with this today?

  The curriculum targets people who may be in active crisis, rebuilding stability, or navigating complex systems without support. That means:

  - Brevity with density: Short lessons that say a lot. No filler.
  - Trauma-aware but not clinical: Acknowledge difficulty without pathologizing.
  - Respect over charity: Treat the learner as capable and intelligent.
  - Practical over theoretical: Every concept must have a real-world application.
  - Progressive mastery: Lessons build on each other. Nothing floats alone.


  ### Lesson Quality Standard

  Every lesson should meet this bar before it ships:

  | Standard     | Description |
  |---|---|
  | Focused      | One clear idea per lesson. Not two. Not three. One. |
  | Actionable   | Learner leaves knowing what to DO, not just what to know |
  | Honest       | Acknowledge real difficulty; don't sugarcoat or minimize |
  | Scenario-grounded | At least one question rooted in a real-life situation |
  | Appropriately challenging | Not too easy (patronizing) or too hard (alienating) |
  | Well-sequenced | Card flow: concept → application → scenario → mission |


  ### Target Card Count Ranges

  | Lesson Type                | Target Cards | Notes |
  |---|---|---|
  | Standard lesson            | 5–7          | Default for all learn-type lessons |
  | Advanced/high-stakes       | 7–9          | Justified by content depth only |
  | Checkpoint lesson          | 8–12         | Mix of review, scenario, application |
  | Capstone lesson            | 10–15        | Path-level synthesis; includes reflection |
  | Intro lesson (first)       | 4–6          | Orient learner; build confidence |

  Current average: 2.7 cards/lesson. Target average: 6 cards/lesson.


  ### Card Type Definitions

  | Card Type       | Purpose |
  |---|---|
  | INFO            | Teach a concept. Trusted-friend tone, not textbook. |
  | MULTIPLE_CHOICE | Test comprehension with 4 options (1 correct, 3 plausible-but-wrong) |
  | TRUE_FALSE      | Quick check on a common misconception |
  | SCENARIO        | Present a real situation; ask what the learner would do |
  | REFLECTION      | Prompt personal thinking — no right/wrong answer |
  | MISSION         | Give a concrete real-world action to take |


  ### Mastery and Checkpoints

  Every Unit ends with a Checkpoint Lesson (8–12 cards) that:
  - Reviews key concepts from all lessons in the unit
  - Includes 2–3 scenario questions
  - Includes 1 reflection card
  - Awards 15 XP (vs. standard 10 XP)

  Every Path ends with a Capstone Lesson (10–15 cards) that:
  - Synthesizes the full path learning
  - Includes a scenario spanning multiple units
  - Includes a reflection and personal commitment card
  - Awards 25 XP

  ## 5. Gold Standard Lesson Template

  Card 1 — INFO
    Prompt: [One-sentence headline — the core truth]
    Body: [2–4 sentences. What is this? Why does it matter? Make it real.]

  Card 2 — INFO (optional second concept if needed)
    Prompt: [Second point or complication]
    Body: [2–3 sentences]

  Card 3 — MULTIPLE_CHOICE
    Prompt: [Comprehension check on concept just taught]
    4 choices: 1 correct, 3 plausible-but-wrong
    Explain: [Why is this right? 1–2 sentences.]

  Card 4 — SCENARIO
    Prompt: [Real situation, 2–3 sentences. Name optional. Make it feel real.]
    4 choices: 1 clearly best, 3 understandable-but-problematic
    Explain: [Connect scenario back to lesson concept.]

  Card 5 — TRUE_FALSE
    Prompt: [Common misconception stated as fact]
    Explain: [Correct the misconception concisely.]

  Card 6 — MULTIPLE_CHOICE
    Prompt: [Application or edge-case question]

  Card 7 — MISSION
    Prompt: Mission: [Action Title]
    Body: [1–3 concrete steps. Present tense. Do today or this week.]

  ## 8. Naming Conventions

  Paths: Noun-phrase, 2–4 words. Slug: kebab-case.
    Examples: Financial Literacy → financial-literacy | Career Launch & Income Building → career-launch

  Modules: Descriptive noun-phrase, 2–4 words.
    Examples: Banking Basics | Crisis to Plan

  Lessons: Start with verb OR specific noun. Avoid "Introduction" or question format.
    Good: Reading a Pay Stub | What Credit Actually Is
    Bad: Introduction | What Is Credit?

  Card prompts:
    INFO: Noun-phrase headline statement
    MULTIPLE_CHOICE/TRUE_FALSE: Complete sentence question
    MISSION: Always starts with "Mission:"
    REFLECTION: Always starts with "Reflect:"
